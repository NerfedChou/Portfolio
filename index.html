Five Nights at Malacañang

Inspired by the countless corruptions in the Philippines, I decide to create a game that is simple and thrill using C# and Unity. You are a security guard that is assigned to guard the Malacañang Palace. Your task is to survive the night from 6 PM to 6 AM. You have a limited power supply that you can use to monitor the cameras and close the doors. If you run out of power, you will be vulnerable to the corrupt officials. Your mission is to find the hidden documents that will expose the corrupt officials and escape the palace alive. If you can find the hidden Easter Egg called GHOST PROJECTS you will unlock a special ending. Can you survive the night and expose the corruption?

BEDROOM 2:35 Framework

BEDROOM 2:35 is a revolutionary open-source framework born out of a developer's frustration—a 2 AM realization that current web frameworks are a prison of complexity, forcing creators to spend years memorizing syntax and fighting against rigid code. We reject this. BEDROOM 2:35 is the fundamental inversion of control, designed to solve the biggest problems in front-end development by guaranteeing both Freedom and Assurance. We provide a core set of 10-20 Smart Containers—the unstyled, perfectly responsive LEGO Bricks—that structure your website with minimal code. For styling, we require no new utility language; instead, you use your own Custom Saw—vanilla CSS with simple modifier properties (e.g., --padding). This eliminates the steep learning curve of systems like Tailwind and the design rigidity of Bootstrap, making your development skills immediately productive. Furthermore, our unique Smart Engine works invisibly in the background, converting beginner errors like fixed px units to responsive rem and actively defending the structural integrity of your layout against aggressive JavaScript manipulation. This system guarantees your code will not break, allowing you to fly without having to worry. By offering this solution for free and focusing the developer purely on design mastery, BEDROOM 2:35 is poised to dominate the market by becoming the essential, anti-fragile, and developer-centric foundation for the next generation of the web.
